## Why NovaPharm exists

A pharmaceutical product does not reach a market through regulatory approval alone. It needs a viable manufacturer, the right technical documentation, a workable batch strategy, suitable packaging, dependable sourcing, commercial pricing and a route through the channel.

These decisions are often made by separate teams at separate times. That creates avoidable rework, weak economics and fragile launches. NovaPharm is being built to connect product, regulatory, manufacturing, supply and commercial thinking earlier.

## What NovaPharm is building

### Market access and product strategy

The company evaluates opportunities through clinical relevance, competitive position, regulatory pathway, manufacturing feasibility, supply risk, price, channel fit and working-capital requirements.

### Licensing and regulatory pathways

NovaPharm’s work includes building the regulatory and quality foundations required for product registration, parallel import pathways and responsible market entry across the United Kingdom and selected international markets.

### Manufacturing and technology-transfer partnerships

The company is developing relationships with manufacturers and CMO/CDMO partners capable of supporting formulation transfer, analytical work, stability studies, submission batches, process validation and long-term commercial supply.

### Sourcing and supply architecture

A resilient supply model requires more than finding a product. It requires qualified sources, clear responsibilities, appropriate storage and transport, realistic lead times, batch traceability and contingency planning.

### Commercialisation and channel development

NovaPharm is building the commercial route between the product and the market: pricing, pack strategy, launch sequencing, wholesaler and pharmacy engagement, demand planning and account development.

### Digital operating infrastructure

The longer-term direction includes digital workflows for product information, controlled documents, supplier qualification, inventory visibility, order coordination, traceability and decision support.

## The operating model

NovaPharm’s strategy connects the decisions that determine whether a pharmaceutical opportunity can become a sustainable business:

**Product opportunity → regulatory pathway → manufacturer → supply → market → channel → patient access**

The quality of the route matters as much as the quality of the individual product. A commercially attractive molecule can still fail through the wrong pack, poor batch economics, weak supply continuity or a channel with no reason to switch.

## Geographic direction

The United Kingdom is the company’s primary market and strategic base. Europe forms an important part of the manufacturing, sourcing and market-access landscape. India is relevant to product development, technical partnerships and manufacturing capability. Over time, NovaPharm intends to extend selected products and partnerships into other regulated international markets where the commercial and operational case is strong.

## Founder role

Vishal leads company strategy, product selection, market-access planning, manufacturing and commercial partnerships, supply development and the broader operating model.

His focus is on turning opportunity into a sequence that can be executed: choosing the right product, finding the right partner, establishing the right route and building the commercial conditions required for durable market access.
