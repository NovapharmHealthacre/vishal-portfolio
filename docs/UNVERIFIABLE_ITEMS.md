# Unverifiable and excluded items

Reviewed: 14 July 2026

The following items are not published as affirmative facts because evidence, current status, public-safety approval or counterparty permission is incomplete.

## Identity and biography

- Nationality, residence and location-based founder wording.
- Full legal name outside a necessary legal context.
- Education, qualifications, awards and professional memberships not independently verified.
- A specific SyriMed job title or confidential responsibilities.
- Personal net worth, family information, date of birth, passport or immigration status.
- Unverified X, Instagram, YouTube, personal GitHub, Crunchbase, Wikipedia or Wikidata profiles.
- Investor, adviser, host or established speaker labels.

## NovaPharm status

- MHRA wholesale authorisation or other regulatory licences.
- GDP certification or inspection status.
- PLPI holdings or approved products.
- NHS contracts, customers, suppliers or active distribution.
- Revenue, investment, break-even, hiring or market-scale achievements.
- Polar Speed or other logistics relationships.
- Deployed B2B, AI, blockchain, pharmacovigilance or forecasting systems.
- Live oncology or specialist-medicine portfolio.
- Operational CMO/CDMO relationships.
- Exact current India entity name, legal relationship and status.

## Search and authority

- Exact Google or Bing index coverage without owner-console access.
- A confirmed Knowledge Panel.
- Complete backlink or referring-domain counts.
- AI citations or training inclusion.
- Rich-result eligibility beyond successful syntax/semantic validation.
- Google Discover eligibility or traffic.
- Search rankings or future traffic outcomes.

## Operational data

- Public inbox deliverability beyond owner confirmation.
- Email retention, deletion, disclosure and legal-basis commitments not operationally documented.
- Search Console and Bing verification/account status.
- Real-user Core Web Vitals until sufficient field data exists.

## Publication rule

An item can move from this list only when:

1. current authoritative evidence exists;
2. the fact ledger is updated;
3. the wording is public-safe and proportionate;
4. counterparty naming permission exists where relevant;
5. visible content, schema and machine-readable facts agree;
6. owner approval is recorded when required.

Private source documents are not made public merely to strengthen SEO.
