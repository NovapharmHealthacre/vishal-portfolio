# Content strategy

## Positioning

Primary public proposition:

> Founder and operator working on more resilient access, licensing and supply systems for regulated healthcare markets.

This wording is intentionally active but non-absolute. It describes Vishal's area of work without claiming that NovaPharm already holds licences, supplies customers or operates deployed infrastructure.

## Narrative hierarchy

1. Who Vishal is now: Founder & CEO of a UK-registered company.
2. What he works on: access, licensing and supply questions in regulated healthcare.
3. What shaped the work: pharmaceutical experience predating NovaPharm, including SyriMed work from 2020 to 2025.
4. What NovaPharm is today: an active UK-registered company incorporated in September 2025.
5. What is still a plan: regulated operations, specialist-medicine model, digital workflow and traceability research.
6. Where independent evidence exists: Companies House and Yakuji Nippo.
7. What to read or discuss next: essays, selected speaking themes and contact.

## Voice

- British English.
- First person for founder perspective; third person for facts and press copy.
- Short, concrete sentences.
- Calm commercial language with explicit qualifiers.
- Explain acronyms on first use.
- Never conflate personal experience, company registration, regulatory permission and current operation.

## Page purposes

### Home

An orientation layer, not a complete biography. It states the proposition, introduces current work, labels venture status, surfaces selected writing and sends evidence-seeking visitors to `/facts/`.

### About

Canonical founder profile with professional history, operating principles, selected milestones and official links. No immigration, nationality, residence or unnecessary personal detail.

### Ventures

One verified organisation only until the India entity is legally resolved. A three-part status presentation separates registered facts, current focus and roadmap.

### Thinking

Editorial index generated from content. Each essay includes author, published/modified dates, reading time, category, disclaimer, primary sources, related work and next/previous navigation.

### Media

Verified Yakuji contribution record with three published instalments in English and Japanese and a planned-fourth status note. No fake press wall.

### Speaking & partnerships

Topics Vishal is available to discuss, not a claim of prior engagements. Enquiry paths cover regulated-market building, UK-EU market access and founder operating systems.

### Facts

Journalist- and machine-readable entity page with short bios, company facts, status definitions, official links, last-reviewed date and corrections channel.

### Contact

Direct email and verified LinkedIn. No paid form provider, tracking or unnecessary data collection.

## Bio set

### 40 and 100 words

The canonical short and medium biographies live in `src/data/entity.mjs`, render on `/facts/`, and are covered by exact 40- and 100-word tests. Do not duplicate their wording here; update the entity source, fact ledger and tests together.

### 250 words

Use the 100-word biography as the public default. A longer biography should be released only after the owner confirms professional history, current location-neutral wording, portrait rights and any additional milestones. This prevents a padded biography from turning projections into credentials.

## Editorial policy

- Preserve author voice while removing unsupported biography and market claims.
- Link pharmaceutical claims to current primary sources.
- State dates and distinguish commentary from factual guidance.
- No patient-specific medical advice.
- No unreviewed product, route-of-administration or clinical claims.
- Display a content-level “last reviewed” date.
- Update or withdraw an essay when primary guidance materially changes.

## Publishing cadence

Quality over volume. A sustainable target is one primary-source-backed essay every four to six weeks, plus factual-page verification once per quarter or upon material company/regulatory change.

## Evidence language

- `Current`: verified public record or first-party source.
- `In progress`: documented current work without completed outcome.
- `Roadmap`: intended future capability.
- `Last reviewed`: date the evidence was checked, not an implied operational certification.
