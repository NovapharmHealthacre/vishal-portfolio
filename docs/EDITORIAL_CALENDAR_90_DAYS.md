# 90-day editorial calendar

Planning window: 15 July to 13 October 2026

This is a briefing calendar, not automatic publishing authority. Dates are targets and should move when evidence or review is incomplete.

## Weeks 1–2: consolidate and distribute existing authority

### Existing essay: Building in Regulated Industries

- Objective: establish the central founder thesis that regulation is an operating system.
- Action: verify current MHRA source links and distribute through LinkedIn.
- Visual concept: a four-layer diagram showing entity, permission, process and evidence.
- Discussion question: Where does your organisation still treat compliance as a final gate rather than an operating input?

### Existing essay: What Parallel Import Actually Means

- Objective: provide the clearest public explanation of commercial observation versus regulated route.
- Action: review Windsor Framework references before distribution.
- Visual concept: source product → UK reference → licence → authorised supply chain.
- Discussion question: Which part of parallel import is most commonly mistaken for a shortcut?

## Weeks 3–5: new article brief 1

### Working title

Regulatory Approval Is Not Market Access

### Original value

A six-question sequencing framework connecting approval, repeatable manufacture, compliant supply, sustainable price, channel adoption and patient access.

### Sources

MHRA, NHS/NICE where relevant, official UK market-access guidance and clearly labelled founder analysis.

### Boundary

Do not imply NovaPharm has launched products or completed approvals.

## Weeks 6–8: new article brief 2

### Working title

The Supplier Qualification Decision Most Teams Make Too Late

### Original value

A public-safe framework for separating commercial attractiveness, regulatory suitability, quality evidence, operational fit and continuity risk.

### Sources

MHRA GDP/GMP guidance, ICH quality-risk principles and official supply-chain guidance.

### Boundary

No supplier names, scores, private audit methods or confidential thresholds.

## Weeks 9–11: new article brief 3

### Working title

Technology Transfer Starts Before the Formula Moves

### Original value

A sequencing model for product knowledge, analytical methods, batch scale, packaging, stability, responsibilities and commercial assumptions in oral-liquid projects.

### Sources

ICH quality guidance, official GMP guidance and appropriately public technical references.

### Boundary

No molecule-specific confidential project details or CMO identities.

## Weeks 12–13: evidence and correction cycle

- Review public facts and publication status.
- Add the fourth Yakuji instalment only if a live publisher URL exists.
- Review existing essay citations for redirects or material regulatory changes.
- Compare Search Console snippets with approved metadata.
- Decide which new article brief has enough evidence and original value to draft next.

## Publishing checklist

Before any article is published:

- author approval;
- fact-ledger review;
- primary-source review;
- title/description/canonical review;
- medical and regulatory scope review;
- internal links;
- social image and alt text;
- material publication date;
- complete release tests.
