# Knowledge Panel readiness

Reviewed: 14 July 2026

## Current finding

No reliable Google or Bing Knowledge Panel for Vishal Chakravarty was confirmed through the available public-search interface during this audit.

A Knowledge Panel cannot be created or guaranteed by adding schema. Google generates panels automatically from information across multiple public sources.

## Readiness foundation now present

- One canonical Person identifier.
- One canonical personal website and ProfilePage.
- A consistent professional name.
- A verified current role and founder relationship.
- A high-quality crawlable portrait.
- A public facts page and machine-readable facts record.
- Verified LinkedIn identity only in `sameAs`.
- Companies House organisation evidence.
- Publisher-hosted Yakuji publication URLs.
- Stable canonical routes and structured data.
- Correction contact and material review dates.

## Remaining authority gaps

- Limited independent biographical coverage about Vishal as the subject.
- No confirmed broad media profile or established speaker record.
- No approved additional social profiles.
- Corporate website still needs to publish the agreed Organization identifier.
- Search engines may retain old or conflicting legacy descriptions while recrawling.
- Authored articles demonstrate expertise but are not independent notability evidence.

## Owner checklist if a correct panel appears

1. Confirm the panel represents the correct Vishal Chakravarty.
2. Use Google’s claim/feedback process to verify representative status.
3. Select an accurate, owner-approved portrait.
4. Suggest the canonical personal website and verified LinkedIn profile.
5. Correct role, organisation, image or biography errors with authoritative evidence.
6. Do not submit private documents unless Google’s secure process explicitly requires them and their use is understood.
7. Keep a dated record of suggestions and outcomes.

## Prohibited tactics

- Creating Wikipedia or Wikidata solely for SEO.
- Adding circular self-controlled citations.
- Purchasing press coverage or links.
- Creating mass directory profiles.
- Fabricating awards, roles, articles or social accounts.
- Adding unrelated people with the same name to `sameAs`.
- Treating a Google Business Profile as a personal Knowledge Panel strategy.

## Monitoring

Review quarterly using branded searches and Search Console. A panel should be claimed only when it exists and is clearly the correct entity.
