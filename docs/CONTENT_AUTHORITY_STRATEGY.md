# Personal content-authority strategy

Reviewed: 14 July 2026

## Position

The portfolio should earn authority through precise, first-hand founder analysis rather than volume. The editorial promise is: complex regulated-market questions explained with clear status boundaries, primary sources and operating judgement.

## Audience

- Pharmaceutical founders and operators
- Manufacturers and CMO/CDMO leaders
- Regulatory and quality professionals
- Market-access and supply-chain teams
- Journalists and editors covering regulated business
- Investors or partners evaluating operating discipline
- Founders building in other high-barrier sectors

## Authority pillars

1. Building in regulated markets
2. UK–EU pharmaceutical market access
3. Pharmaceutical supply resilience
4. Post-Brexit regulatory-commercial sequencing
5. Parallel import explained responsibly
6. CMO/CDMO and technology-transfer decisions
7. Supplier qualification and operating discipline
8. Evidence-led founder systems
9. Cross-border pharmaceutical partnerships
10. Credibility before scale
11. Resilience, judgement and founder operations
12. Responsible data and technology use in regulated industries

## Content standard

Every proposed article must provide:

- an original operating insight, framework or decision model;
- a clearly defined professional audience;
- a concise answer or thesis near the beginning;
- primary sources for regulatory or factual claims;
- visible author, publication date and material update date;
- fact/opinion/future-intention separation;
- a practical consequence for the reader;
- a reason the article belongs to Vishal’s personal platform rather than a generic corporate blog.

## Preferred formats

- Decision frameworks
- Risk and dependency maps
- Operating checklists
- Sequencing models
- Myth-versus-reality analysis
- Post-mortem or correction notes
- Source-led explainers
- Founder field notes
- Annotated process diagrams using public-safe abstractions

## Exclusions

Do not publish patient advice, medicine-use guidance, unapproved product availability, confidential supplier information, pricing, commercial forecasts, private regulatory strategy, copied business-plan material or mass AI-generated summaries.

## Editorial mix

- 50% regulated-market operating insight
- 25% pharmaceutical market access and supply analysis
- 15% founder systems and decision quality
- 10% corrections, public evidence and lessons from building

## Quality over cadence

Target one substantial article every four to six weeks. A shorter field note may be added between major essays only when it contributes an original idea. Do not publish to satisfy a calendar.

## Distribution

Each article should receive:

1. canonical website publication;
2. one personal LinkedIn post with an original hook;
3. one visual framework concept;
4. one discussion question;
5. direct sharing only with genuinely relevant contacts;
6. selective editorial pitching where the article offers independent value.

## Measurement

Use Search Console, Bing Webmaster, LinkedIn analytics and qualitative enquiry evidence. Do not add website tracking in this phase.
