# Editorial policy

Effective: 14 July 2026

## Purpose

The personal founder platform publishes analysis on regulated markets, pharmaceutical access and founder operations. The objective is useful, source-led thinking, not medical advice or promotional volume.

## Author identity

The standard public author name is Vishal Chakravarty. Articles must reference the canonical Person identifier and display the author visibly.

## Source standard

Regulatory and pharmaceutical factual claims should use primary sources where available, including GOV.UK, MHRA, EMA, European Commission, ICH, WHO, official registries and relevant peer-reviewed material.

Competitor marketing pages are not primary authority when an official source exists. Substantial source text must not be copied.

## Fact, opinion and intention

Articles must distinguish:

- current verified facts;
- historical verified facts;
- analysis or opinion;
- work in progress;
- future plans;
- unresolved or private information.

Company registration must not be described as regulatory authorisation. Plans, relationships and technologies must not be described as operating achievements.

## Medical and regulatory scope

The site does not provide patient-specific medical advice, diagnosis, treatment or medicine-use instructions. General regulatory analysis does not replace current official guidance or qualified legal, regulatory, quality or healthcare advice.

The phrase “medically reviewed” must not be used unless an appropriately qualified reviewer has genuinely completed and approved that review.

## Technical review

A specialist reviewer should be used when an article makes material technical claims outside the author’s safely supportable public record. The reviewer’s name, role and scope must be verified and approved before publication.

## Corrections

Material errors should be corrected promptly. The page should receive a new modification date only when content is materially changed. A correction note should be added when the prior error could have influenced interpretation.

Factual correction requests may be sent to the public contact inbox with primary evidence where possible.

## Conflicts of interest

Commercial relationships relevant to an article should be disclosed when public, current and material. Confidential relationships must not be implied or named without permission.

## AI-assisted drafting

AI tools may assist with research organisation, outlining, editing or quality checks. They do not replace author approval, source verification, regulatory judgement or originality. No article should be published solely because a model generated it.

## Publication checklist

- original thesis and intended audience;
- author approval;
- primary sources checked and accessible;
- fact ledger updated where necessary;
- title, description, canonical and social image;
- publication and material modification dates;
- internal links and related content;
- disclaimer where relevant;
- structured data matches visible content;
- full repository checks pass.
